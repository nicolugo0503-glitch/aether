# Aether — Pitch

## One-liner

Aether is the operating system for an autonomous AI workforce — teams "hire" specialized AI employees (SDRs, researchers, support reps, analysts) the same way they'd hire humans, billed per seat.

## The problem

Every company in 2026 has the same problem: the work is expanding faster than the headcount. The generic-chatbot era (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) solved the "copy-paste assistant" layer but did not solve *work*. Teams still:

- Context-switch into a chat window to do individual tasks
- Re-enter company context on every prompt
- Fail to operationalize AI across a workflow because it has no memory, no tools, no accountability

Meanwhile, the first generation of "AI agent" companies either (a) ship demos that don't survive production, or (b) require months of consulting to stand up.

## The insight

People understand "hiring". They understand job descriptions, performance, and seat-based pricing. Rethinking AI agents as *employees* — with names, roles, onboarding (knowledge), and performance reviews (runs dashboard) — collapses the onboarding friction to zero.

## The product

1. **Employee catalog** — pre-built AI employees for the highest-ROI roles (SDR, researcher, support, analyst, recruiter, ops). Clone and customize.
2. **Agent runtime** — typed tools, retrieval over company data, function-calling, evaluation harness, audit logs.
3. **Integrations** — wired into the tools employees already use (Gmail, Slack, Salesforce, Notion, HubSpot, Zendesk).
4. **Per-seat billing** — $49 / $299 / $1,499 monthly tiers, with clear run limits. Finance teams already know how to buy seats.

## Market

- US knowledge workers: ~150M
- If only 10% of their employers spend $100/mo on "AI augmentation" per seat → **$18B/year** US-only ARR
- Global TAM on the same math: ~$90B/year
- Analogous SaaS categories (CRM, ATS, helpdesk) each produce multiple billion-dollar companies. "AI workforce" is the category above all of them.

## Why now

- Model quality finally good enough for non-toy work (2024 onward)
- Tool-use reliability good enough for production
- Every CFO has been told to find AI efficiency
- The buyer (VP Sales, VP Support, Head of Ops) is bought in — the question is just "which vendor"

## Competition

- **Artisan, 11x** — single-role AI SDR products. Correct wedge, but narrow.
- **Sierra, Decagon** — AI support. Strong in a single vertical.
- **Cognition (Devin)** — AI engineer. Adjacent, same pattern.
- **Salesforce Agentforce, Microsoft Copilot Studio** — platform plays, but bolted onto legacy suites.

**Why Aether wins:** multi-role catalog + neutral stance + 10-minute onboarding + consumer-grade UX. The incumbents can't move fast; the single-role startups can't expand without rebuilding.

## Business model

- Seats: $49 / $299 / $1,499 per workspace per month
- Overage: metered $/1k tokens above plan limits
- Enterprise: $50k-$500k ACV with SSO, SCIM, BYO-model, VPC deployment, CSM

Gross margin is 70-80% at steady state (OpenAI passthrough + infra).

## Traction hypothesis

- **Weeks 1–12:** land 25 design-partner workspaces at $299/mo → $90k ARR
- **Months 3–12:** reach $1M ARR via performance marketing + SDR playbook
- **Year 2:** enterprise motion + verticalized employees → $10M ARR
- **Year 3:** category definition + platform expansion → $50M+ ARR
